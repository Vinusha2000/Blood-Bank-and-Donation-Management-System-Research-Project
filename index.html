Research In Detail

Literature Survey


The development of a web-based Blood Bank and Donation Management System (BBDMS) encompasses various components aimed at enhancing the efficiency, security, organization, and predictive capabilities of blood banks. This integrates the findings from multiple studies to provide a comprehensive overview of the existing research and advancements in these areas.

  • Efficient Blood Supply and Demand Management.
  • Data Security with Blockchain.
  • Location-Based Appointment Scheduling and Blood Camp Analysis.
  • Predicting Potential Blood Donors.

The Blood Bank and Donation Management System (BBDMS) is a web-based system designed to improve blood bank efficiency, security, organization, and predictive capabilities. It uses advanced deep learning models for demand forecasting, blockchain technology for data security, and Google Maps for appointment scheduling. The system also analyzes blood type, improves blood camp quality, and predicts potential donors based on demographic factors. The BBDMS project uses innovative technologies to enhance blood bank and donor management systems, promoting efficiency, security, data-driven decision-making, and user experience.

Research Gap

Significant deficiencies in the current blood banking systems have been revealed and a thorough study has been conducted on how to avoid them using new technology.

The system promotes inter-hospital collaboration, facilitates resource sharing, and addresses ethical considerations and data privacy. This research aims to bridge gaps in blood demand forecasting, integration with healthcare systems, inter-hospital collaboration, and ethical data handling, ultimately enhancing blood resource management and healthcare practices.

The study identifies flaws in the Donor Management System for Blood Donation Management (BBDM) and current blood bank systems, including a lack of a blockchain-based system, Google Map location-finding system, and organization-requesting system. It suggests implementing donor review analysis to improve donor experience and optimize blood collection efforts. The study also suggests advances in blood demand forecasting using 1D Convolutional Neural Networks for high-granularity forecasting. The system promotes inter-hospital collaboration, facilitates resource sharing, and addresses ethical considerations and data privacy.

Research Problem & Solution

This study focuses on the challenges in blood donation due to the dynamic nature of patients' medical information. The process of eligibility examination is time-consuming and inefficient, making it difficult to allocate resources efficiently. The lack of donor attendance predictability is a significant problem in blood camps, and developing models for predicting donor participation can improve planning. A user-friendly platform can reduce wait times and optimize the blood donation process. Data-driven decision-making, community engagement, and inclusive design are also crucial. The study also addresses the challenges of managing blood emergencies in healthcare, including forecasting blood demand, timely procurement, real-time inventory management, ethical data handling, resource optimization, mobile blood camp coordination, effective communication, and post-emergency blood inventory recovery.

  • How to manage blood demand in an emergency?
  • How to enhance blood banks through data security methods for sensitive data?
  • How do we analyze blood camp details and organizations efficiently?
  • How to predict future blood donation for needs?

The study developed a web-based Blood Bank and Donation Management System (BBDMS) using machine learning models and technologies. The system uses a 1D Convolutional Neural Network for accurate blood demand forecasting, blockchain for data security, a Random Forest Classifier for donor eligibility assessment, GRU-based models for text classification and time series forecasting, and data-driven approaches for donor recruitment. These advancements optimize blood donation management, increase productivity, and reduce operational costs.

Research Objectives

Main Objective

Enhancing Blood Supply Chain Management and Data Security for Effective Blood Emergency Response and Demand Forecasting in Healthcare Systems .

Sub Objective 1 :

Develop accurate forecasting methods for varying blood demand.

Sub Objective 2 :

Data security for sensitive information of blood bank and donor management system using blockchain.

Sub Objective 3 :

Develop a robust system for blood camp organization,donor appointment management, and predictive blood camp details forecasting.

Sub Objective 4 :

Develop a model for predict whether doner will donate blood in next year or not.

Methodology

The Web-Based Blood Bank and Donation Management System (BBDMS) utilizes Python, a MySQL database, Flask Rest API, and React.js for a web-based system. It employs 1D Convolutional Neural Networks for accurate demand prediction, Gated Recurrent Unit models for Blood Camp and Appointment Reviewing, and Random Forest, XGBoost, K-Neighbors, and Support Vector Classification models for donor eligibility assessment. The system uses advanced encryption, access control,blockchain technology, and predictive analytics for secure blood donation management and improved healthcare resource allocation. ‘Blood Bank Donation Management System’ consists of four main components. Those four components are as follows:

  • Blood Demand Forecasting.
  • Data Security and Role Base Access Controlling.
  • Appointment Forecasting and Blood Camps Analysis.
  • Prediction of Future Blood Donations.

The Web-Based Blood Bank and Donation Management System (BBDMS) methodology focuses on the creation and training of ML models, using cutting-edge algorithms and iterative experimentation for optimization. We create a user-friendly interface for healthcare practitioners to enter donor data and get forecasts to make practical implementation possible. Following are rigorous validation and testing stages that evaluate the model.
